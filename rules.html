Shogi, or Japanese Chess, is a strategy game where captured pieces can be reused, creating endless tactical possibilities.

How to Play:

  • Each player commands 20 pieces, including a king, generals, and pawns.
  • Captured pieces can be dropped back onto the board as your own.
  • Pieces can be promoted when they enter the enemy zone.
  • Win by checkmating your opponent‚Äôs king.

‚öΩ Football

Football (soccer) is played between two teams of 11 players each, aiming to score goals using a round ball.

How to Play:

  • Matches last 90 minutes, split into two halves.
  • Players can use any part of their body except their hands and arms (goalkeepers can).
  • A goal is scored when the ball crosses the goal line completely.
  • The team with the most goals wins.

üèè Cricket

Cricket is a team sport involving batting, bowling, and fielding. Teams take turns scoring runs and defending wickets.

How to Play:

  • Two teams of 11 players each take turns batting and bowling.
  • Batters score runs by hitting the ball and running between wickets.
  • Fielders aim to dismiss batters through catches, run-outs, or bowling.
  • The team with the most runs after both innings wins.
